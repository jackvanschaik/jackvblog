Indiana Coronavirus Case Model

I’ve been working on a statistical model for predicting daily COVID-19 case counts in Indiana.

I gathered data from 7 places:

  • https://github.com/RamiKrispin/coronavirus
  • https://www.coronavirus.in.gov/2393.htm
  • https://trends.google.com/trends/?geo=US
  • https://evictionlab.org/
  • https://www.gstatic.com/covid19/mobility/Global_Mobility_Report.csv
  • https://www.wfyi.org/
  • https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/rural-urban-commuting-area-codes/

I also manually included the “phases” for Holcomb’s Back on Track plan.

Results

My model is still in development, but I’ve made some progress. I set aside the last 28 days as testing data and trained my model on the remaining data.

After some tweaking, here are two iterations that were fairly successful:

Version 1

This version of the model has testing \(R^2\) of about 0.714. It significantly underpredicts cases in the most recent week, however.

Version 2

This next version of the model has a lower testing \(R^2\) of 0.634, however it is simpler than the above model, more interpretable, and captures the overall trend pretty well.

Discussion

While these results are preliminary and the model is not yet very sophisticated, I have been able to make some inferences:

  • Day of week is needed to account for weekly periodicity
  • Google searches for “Indiana Coronavirus” are somewhat predictive of COVID-19 cases
  • Mobility, both on the county and state level, are predictive of COVID-19 cases

I’ll go into more detail later. I also hope to do daily forecasting.
